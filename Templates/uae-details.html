United Arab Emirates

  • Religion: Islam
  • Currency: UAE Dirhams
  • Official Language: Arabic
  • Calling Code: +971

UAE or Emirates, known as the most popular tourist destination in the world.

The United Arab Emirates is an elective monarchy formed from a federation of seven emirates, consisting of Abu Dhabi (the capital), Ajman, Dubai, Fujairah, Ras Al Khaimah, Sharjah and Umm Al Quwain.

City Destinations

Dubai

  • Modern Architectural Marvels
  • Global business and tourism hub
  • Cultural Diversity and Cosmopolitanism
  • Luxurious lifestyle and shopping

The City of Dreams

Dubai is a vibrant metropolis known for its towering skyscrapers, luxurious resorts, and dynamic blend of cultures.Dubai is a dynamic city that combines modernity with traditional Arabian charm. Its awe-inspiring architecture, thriving business environment, diverse tourist attractions, and multicultural society make it a truly global destination.

Abu Dhabi

  • Capital of UAE
  • Modern skyline, traditional heritage
  • Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
  • Ferrari World Abu Dhabi

Garden City of the Gulf

Abu Dhabi, the capital city of the United Arab Emirates, is a modern metropolis known for its opulent architecture, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage. With its gleaming skyscrapers, including the iconic Etihad Towers, and luxurious waterfront resorts, Abu Dhabi showcases the perfect blend of contemporary development and traditional Arabian charm.

Sharjah

  • Art and Heritage Hub
  • Academic Excellence Center
  • Biodiversity Conservation Efforts
  • Manufacturing and Trading Hub

Cultural Capital of the UAE

Sharjah is renowned for its commitment to preserving and promoting Arab culture and heritage through its numerous museums, art galleries, and cultural festivals. The city boasts a diverse range of attractions, including stunning mosques, traditional souks, and beautiful corniches. With its emphasis on education and academic excellence, Sharjah is also home to several prestigious universities and educational institutions.
